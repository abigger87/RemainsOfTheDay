Kazuo Ishiguro

Kazuo Ishiguro was born on November 8, 1954. He writes English novels, short stories, and screenplays. When he was 5 years old, he moved from his birthplace of Nagasaki, Japan to England in 1960. He is a recognized as a prominent fiction author, receiving four Man Booker Prize nominations and winning the 1989 award for The Remains of the Day. In 2017 the Swedish Academy awarded him with the Nobel Prize in Literature.
